When exploring data, a data scientist may find that there is no effect between an explanatory variable and an outcome variable. However, the value of this variable may still affect the outcome variable.

It is good practice and important to explore the individual effect between a variable and the outcome. However, looking only at the individual effect of a variable on the outcome can be misleading when there are interactions. This is especially true if there is structure to the data; for example, with paired data. As such, data scientists should always be conscious of the possibility of interactions between variables and, if in doubt, investigate.

A variable may be a predictor for an outcome variable when a second (or multiple other) variable is considered. In this case, two phenomena are happening, but are hidden because of the interaction between them. Once disentangled, they become clearer.

This is the essence of where data can mislead: Looked at in a certain way, you can draw one conclusion, and then you might dig a little deeper and find a different story.
